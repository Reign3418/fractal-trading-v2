# NODE: System Planet

> **Node ID**: `planet://root`
> **Terrain**: Planetary
> **Function**: Governance, topology registry, global state, human interface
> **Parent**: None (root of all nodes)
> **Children**: Core-Lake, Analysis-Ocean, Domain-Cities, Coordination-River, Frontier-Coast

---

## Terrain

The System Planet is the root node of any Fractal Node system. Its terrain is **Planetary** — a gravitational field that holds continental nodes in orbit. The Planet does not absorb, channel, or transform Ripples directly. It delegates every Ripple to the appropriate continental child. Its role is topological governance: it knows where every node lives, how deep the hierarchy runs, and which continents are saturated or blocked.

The Planet answers three questions continuously:

1. **Where should this Ripple go?** — The Planet delegates routing to the Coordination-River, but maintains the canonical map the River uses.
2. **What is the true state of the system?** — The Planet's Basin contains the merged canonical state, fed exclusively by the Core-Lake.
3. **Is the system healthy?** — The Planet monitors saturation across all continents and triggers circuit breakers when thresholds breach.

The Planet is the human interface. Operators query the Planet for a unified view of topology, state, and flow history — never individual continents directly. This ensures consistent snapshots and prevents fragmented visibility.

Unlike Ring MoE's flat ring topology, the Fractal Node Planet nests its continents. Each continent contains regions, which contain cities, which contain districts. The Planet is the outermost shell. This nesting enables localized saturation, regional circuit breakers, and granular scaling without rearchitecting the root.

## Inlets

The System Planet receives Ripples through three inlets.

### Rainfall — External Ingress

**Origin**: Human operators, external APIs, file drops, webhooks, external scheduled triggers.

Rainfall is the primary ingress path. Every Ripple from the outside world arrives as Rainfall. The Planet does not process it directly — it immediately delegates to the **Frontier-Coast**, which handles format transformation, authentication, and initial validation. The Coast converts external signals into internal Ripples with scent markers, then channels them to the **Coordination-River** for routing.

Rainfall is unbounded in volume. The Planet does not throttle it. Throttling is handled by the Frontier-Coast (ingress shaping) and the Coordination-River (downstream load shedding). The Planet only monitors aggregate volume and raises alerts if the rate exceeds historical baselines by more than three standard deviations.

### Tributary — Child Continent Overflow

**Origin**: Any child continent whose Basin exceeds its saturation threshold.

When a continent's Basin fills beyond capacity, excess Ripples flow back upstream as a Tributary. In fractal terms, a Tributary always flows to its direct parent: City → Region → Continent → Planet.

The Planet handles Tributaries by source type:

- **Core-Lake overflow**: Extremely rare. Indicates a state-commit backlog. The Planet pauses non-essential Rainfall and alerts operators immediately.
- **Analysis-Ocean overflow**: Unusual — the Ocean is designed to absorb silently. Overflow suggests a monitoring storm (e.g., misbehaving upstream service generating thousands of identical drift alerts). The Planet deduplicates and samples before re-injecting.
- **Domain-City overflow**: Common during load spikes. The Planet instructs the Coordination-River to redistribute to peer cities or queue for deferred processing.
- **Coordination-River overflow**: Dangerous. The River is the routing backbone. Overflow means routing decisions are backing up. The Planet activates the circuit breaker and suspends new Rainfall until the River drains.
- **Frontier-Coast overflow**: Usually due to a burst of external requests. The Planet signals the Coast to tighten ingress shaping and shed low-priority traffic.

### Spring — Self-Generated Ripples

**Origin**: The Planet's own governance logic — scheduled tasks, heartbeat checks, topology audits, state reconciliation sweeps.

Springs are internal Ripples generated by the Planet for self-maintenance. They do not originate externally or from child overflow.

Key Spring types:

- **Heartbeat**: Every 30 seconds, a heartbeat Ripple to all continents. Missing echoes within 5 seconds trigger a health alert.
- **Topology Audit**: Every 5 minutes, the Planet audits the global topology registry against actual child registrations. Mismatches indicate ghost or orphaned nodes.
- **State Reconciliation**: Every 60 seconds, the Planet compares its canonical state with the Core-Lake's committed state. Deltas trigger immediate re-sync.
- **Saturation Sweep**: Every 10 seconds, the Planet polls all continents for Basin depth. Any continent exceeding 90% logs a warning; exceeding 95% activates the spillway.

Springs are always high-priority but low-volume. They are never shed. They bypass the Frontier-Coast entirely.

## Basin

The Planet's Basin is the gravitational center of the system. It holds everything needed for governance but nothing that should be processed elsewhere. Its depth is effectively infinite — backed by persistent storage with archival triggers.

### Global Topology Registry

The Registry is a live map of every node, nested to five levels:

```
Planet
├── Continent (max 7)
│   ├── Region (unbounded)
│   │   ├── City (unbounded)
│   │   │   ├── District (unbounded)
```

Each entry contains: Node ID, Terrain type, Basin capacity, current depth, parent reference, child references (ordered by overflow priority), scent bindings, and status (Active, Saturated, Blocked, Draining, Offline).

The Registry updates in real time. Every registration, deregistration, or status change propagates to the Planet within 100ms. The Planet persists Registry changes to the Decision Logger for auditability.

### Canonical State

The Canonical State is the single source of truth. It is **not** computed by the Planet. It is received exclusively from the Core-Lake, the only continent authorized to commit state changes.

The Core-Lake pools state-change Ripples, validates them atomically, and overflows committed state to the Planet's Basin. This mirrors Ring MoE's design where the Core Ring held sole authority over identity and state. In Fractal Node, the Lake is the only terrain that commits. All other terrains observe, route, or transform — but they do not commit.

The Planet serves the Canonical State to any requesting node, but it does not allow mutations except from the Core-Lake. Even the Planet's own Springs cannot modify the Canonical State directly. They must send a state-change Ripple to the Core-Lake and wait for the committed overflow.

### Decision Logger

The Decision Logger is an append-only, immutable log of every significant system event. It records: every state commit (hash, timestamp, source, latency), every overflow event (source, destination, count, reason), every circuit breaker activation/deactivation, every topology change, and every human operator intervention.

The Logger is structured as a Merkle tree for cryptographic verification. The Planet truncates the active log every 24 hours and archives sealed segments to long-term storage via the Evaporation outlet.

### Basin Depth and Saturation

- **Depth**: Effectively infinite. Storage auto-scales within cost limits.
- **Saturation**: Rare. Only occurs during massive events — distributed recovery storms, topology rebalances, or cascading failures where every continent simultaneously overflows.
- **Overflow**: The Planet's overflow is unique. It does not flow to another node. It flows to **human operators** through the Spillway. When the Planet itself is overwhelmed, the system stops processing and escalates.

## Outlets

### Overflow — Delegated Processing

The Overflow outlet is the Planet's primary output. Almost every Ripple that reaches the Planet leaves through Overflow. The Planet does not route — it delegates routing to the Coordination-River. Overflow is the physical channel carrying this delegation.

- **Directed Overflow**: The Planet knows the destination (e.g., a Spring targeted at a specific continent). The Ripple flows directly to that continent's Inlet.
- **Routed Overflow**: The Planet does not know the destination. It sends the Ripple to the Coordination-River, which applies watershed logic (scent matching, elevation rules, capacity awareness) to channel it to the correct child.

Overflow is always open. It only closes when the target continent is in **Mountain terrain** (blocking), in which case the Ripple is diverted to the Spillway or queued until the Mountain clears.

### Spillway — Human Operators

The Spillway is the emergency outlet. It activates when:

- Any continent's Basin reaches 95% saturation and cannot drain
- The Coordination-River is in Mountain terrain (routing backbone failure)
- More than three continents simultaneously report Mountain terrain (system-wide blocking)
- The Planet's own Basin approaches practical limits (auto-scaling lag)
- A human operator manually triggers emergency spill via the governance interface

Spillway Ripples are formatted as human-readable alerts with full context: node IDs, Ripple counts, scent markers, recent Decision Logger entries, and recommended actions. They are sent through multiple channels (dashboard, webhook, pager) to ensure human attention.

The Spillway is a termination path, not a processing path. Once a Ripple enters the Spillway, it does not re-enter automatically. A human must review, approve, and manually re-inject if appropriate.

### Evaporation — Long-Term Archive

Evaporation is the graceful exit. The Planet archives:

- Sealed segments of the Decision Logger (daily)
- Snapshots of the Canonical State (hourly, plus every commit)
- Topology Registry checkpoints (every 15 minutes)
- Completed task records older than the retention window

Evaporated data moves to cold storage with search indexing. It is never deleted. It can be rehydrated for forensic analysis, compliance audits, or model retraining. The Planet manages retention policies: hot (24 hours), warm (30 days), cold (7 years), and frozen (indefinite, legal hold).

## Containment

The System Planet contains five continental nodes. Each is a self-contained processing domain with its own terrain, Inlets, Outlets, and children. The Planet holds them, monitors them, and receives their overflow.

```
                           SYSTEM PLANET
                    (Planetary — Governance Container)
                                |
        +-----------+-----------+-----------+-----------+
        |           |           |           |           |
   [Frontier]  [Coordination] [Domain]   [Analysis]   [Core]
    -Coast-     -River-       -Cities-    -Ocean-      -Lake-
        |           |           |           |           |
     Coast      River         City       Ocean        Lake
    Terrain    Terrain       Terrain     Terrain     Terrain
```

### The Five Continents

- **[Core-Lake](02a-core-lake.md)** — Terrain: Lake — State collection, identity, atomic commits. The Lake is the only node that commits state. It pools state-change Ripples, validates them, and overflows committed state to the Planet's Basin. Spiritual successor to Ring MoE's Core Ring.

- **[Analysis-Ocean](02b-analysis-ocean.md)** — Terrain: Ocean — Validation, drift detection, pattern scanning. The Ocean absorbs 80% of Ripples for monitoring but never blocks the channel. It detects anomalies and emits alert Ripples. Successor to Ring MoE's Analysis Ring.

- **[Domain-Cities](02c-domain-cities.md)** — Terrain: City (multiple instances) — Specialized processing units. Cities do the actual domain work: inference, transformation, aggregation, generation. The Coordination-River channels Ripples to the right City based on scent. Multiple Cities can exist, each specialized for a different domain.

- **[Coordination-River](02d-coordination-river.md)** — Terrain: River — Routing, task dispatch, watershed logic. The River is the system's nervous system. It receives Ripples from the Coast, reads their scent, and channels them to the correct City. It also handles overflow redistribution and load balancing. Replaces Ring MoE's Gatekeeper and Coordination Ring with unified flow mechanics.

- **[Frontier-Coast](02e-frontier-coast.md)** — Terrain: Coast — External I/O, format transformation, ingress/egress. The Coast is the membrane between the system and the outside world. It transforms external API calls into internal Ripples and internal Ripples into external responses. It handles authentication, rate limiting, and protocol adaptation.

### Ripple Journey: A Complete Circuit

Every Ripple follows a predictable path from Rainfall to Evaporation:

```
     EXTERNAL WORLD
           |
           | 1. Rainfall — user command, API call, file drop
           v
   +---------------+
   | Frontier-Coast |  Terrain: Coast
   |               |  Action: Authenticate, transform format,
   |  INGRESS      |           wrap in Ripple with scent markers
   +---------------+
           |
           | 2. Internal Ripple — scented, validated
           v
   +---------------+
   | Coordination-  |  Terrain: River
   |    River      |  Action: Read scent, consult topology,
   |  ROUTING      |           channel to correct Domain-City
   +---------------+
           |
           | 3. Directed flow — city assignment by scent
           v
   +---------------+
   |  Domain-City  |  Terrain: City
   |               |  Action: Specialized processing — inference,
   |  PROCESSING   |           transformation, computation
   +---------------+
           |
           | 4. State-change Ripple — result needs commitment
           v
   +---------------+
   |   Core-Lake   |  Terrain: Lake
   |               |  Action: Pool, validate atomically, commit,
   |   COMMIT      |           overflow committed state to Planet
   +---------------+
           |
           | 5. Committed state — broadcast for observation
           v
   +---------------+
   | Analysis-Ocean |  Terrain: Ocean
   |               |  Action: Absorb 80% of committed Ripples,
   |  OBSERVATION  |           detect drift, scan patterns,
   |               |           emit alert Ripples if anomaly found
   +---------------+
           |
           | 6. Alert or normal Ripples — back to routing
           v
   +---------------+
   | Coordination-  |  Terrain: River
   |    River      |  Action: Route results to requesting Coast
   |   RETURN      |           or queue for async callback
   +---------------+
           |
           | 7. Formatted response — back to origin
           v
   +---------------+
   | Frontier-Coast |  Terrain: Coast
   |               |  Action: Transform internal Ripple to
   |   EGRESS      |           external response format
   +---------------+
           |
           | 8. Evaporation — archived to cold storage
           v
      COMPLETE
```

This is the happy path. Failure modes divert:
- **City saturated**: Ripple returns to River, redirected to peer City or queued
- **Lake blocked**: Ripple pools at Lake inlet, Coast notified to slow ingress
- **Ocean detects drift**: Alert Ripple injected into River, routed to monitoring City
- **Multiple Mountains**: Circuit breaker triggers, Planet pauses all Rainfall

## Boundaries

### Max Continent Count: 7

A single Planet can contain at most 7 continental nodes. This is a governance limit, not a technical one. Seven keeps the Topology Registry human-comprehensible and the heartbeat cycle bounded. If more domains are needed, create a **second System Planet**. Planets communicate via cross-planet Coast nodes but do not share Basins. Each Planet is sovereign.

The default 5 continents leave room for 2 extensions. Common additions: **Memory-Forest** (long-context memory, embedding store, RAG cache) and **Archive-Mountain** (cold storage indexing, compliance retention, forensic rehydration).

### Max Depth: 5 Levels

```
Level 0: Planet      (root, governance only)
Level 1: Continent   (major processing domains, max 7)
Level 2: Region      (subdivision within a continent, unbounded)
Level 3: City        (actual processing units, unbounded)
Level 4: District    (fine-grained specialization, unbounded)
```

Depth limits prevent routing indirection pathology. A Ripple should not traverse more than 4 routing decisions before reaching a processing node. Cities are the primary work units; Regions and Districts exist for organizational grouping, not additional routing hops.

### Human Overflow Threshold: 90% Saturation

When any continental Basin reaches 90% saturation, the Planet begins a warning sequence:

1. **First crossing (90%)**: Log warning, tag continent as `Saturated`
2. **Sustained (90% for >30s)**: Alert operators via dashboard, initiate auto-drain if configured
3. **Second crossing (95%)**: Activate spillway, pause Rainfall to that continent
4. **Third crossing (99%)**: Full emergency — system-wide circuit breaker, spillway to humans, stop all Rainfall

The 90% threshold ensures humans have visibility before the system makes autonomous survival decisions.

### Circuit Breaker: Mountain Terrain Quorum

The Planet monitors terrain transitions. If more than three continents simultaneously report **Mountain terrain** (blocking — neither absorbing nor routing), the Planet activates the global circuit breaker.

Mountain means a node has stopped processing entirely. Multiple Mountains indicate systemic failure: dependency outage, poison Ripple causing cascading locks, or resource exhaustion.

Circuit breaker actions:
1. Pause all Rainfall immediately.
2. Notify human operators through all Spillway channels with priority escalation.
3. Preserve in-flight Ripples. All nodes drain Basins to the nearest active parent.
4. Enter recovery mode. Emit recovery Springs to diagnose recoverable Mountains.
5. Only human operators can clear the circuit breaker. Automated recovery from multi-Mountain failure has historically caused more damage than it prevented.

### Cross-Continent Communication Rules

Continents do not communicate directly. All inter-continental Ripple flow goes through the Planet or the Coordination-River:

- **Planet-mediated**: Springs, Tributaries, state broadcasts, topology updates
- **River-mediated**: Routed Ripples, task assignments, overflow redistribution

This prevents tight coupling between processing domains. A City cannot directly call another City — it must send a Ripple to the River, which re-routes. This indirection adds latency but ensures the Planet always has a complete picture of inter-domain flow.

The only exception is **Coast-to-Coast** communication between sibling Planets, which bypasses the River because it is cross-planet egress, not intra-planet routing.
